I Can't Believe There's No Gluten (or Dairy) Brownies!

When I gave up gluten and dairy, I thought my baking days were behind me. I wasn't thrilled by the prospect, but I knew that I was committed to my new dietary restrictions. So I resigned myself to a bakeless existence. That is, until I came across the blog post on texanerin.com (another magnificent Erin) that changed my life. A recipe for the ooey-gooiest, fudgy-wudgiest brownies you’ve ever had. And you'd never believe that they’re dairy AND gluten free too! Did I mention that they're totally easy to make too? Seriously, this recipe is a must have in any kitchen, whether you have restrictions or not. Now get ready for some serious decadence and enjoy!

Working Time: 15 min     Baking Time: 20-25 min     Yield: 9

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Bob’s Red Mill Gluten Free 1-to-1 Baking Flour
  • 3/4 cups Dutch-processed cocoa powder
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt
  • 3/4 cups + 2 Tbsp coconut oil, melted and slightly cooled
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1 Tbsp vanilla extract
  • 3 large eggs
  • 3/4 cups semi-sweet dairy-free chocolate chips
  • 1/4 cup more semi-sweet dairy-free chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350° F (175° C) and line an 8”x8” pan with parchemnt paper.
  2. In a medium mixing bowl, stir together the flour, cocoa powder, and salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large mixing bowl, stir together the coconut oil, sugar, and vanilla extract.
  4. Add the eggs one at a time and stir until combined.
  5. Add the dry ingredients to the sugar mix and stir until almost no streaks of flour remain. Do not overmix!
  6. Fold in 3/4 cups of chocolate chips.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and sprinkle the remaining 1/4 cup chocolate chips on top.
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the brownies have formed a thin crust and appear set in the middle.
  9. Let cool completely, then cover and store at room temperature for up to 4 days. You can also refigerate them to give them a fudgier texture.
